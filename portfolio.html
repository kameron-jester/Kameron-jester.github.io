Final Science Photography Portfolio

This portfolio is the culmination of my work in LSC 450: Documentary Photography for the Sciences, where I explored the intersection of visual storytelling and scientific communication. Throughout the semester, I honed my technical skills, experimented with new techniques, and studied the work of influential photographers. This course challenged me to step outside my comfort zone, think more critically about composition and narrative, and use photography as a tool to capture the beauty and complexity of scientific subjects. This portfolio reflects my growth as a photographer and my ability to convey science through a compelling visual lens.

Voices of Consolation: Reagan’s Challenger Address and Obama’s Sandy Hook Speech

This project, completed for my CA 610 course *Theoretical Advances in U.S. Presidential Discourse*, explores the evolution of presidential rhetoric through a comparative analysis of Ronald Reagan’s Challenger Address and Barack Obama’s Sandy Hook speech. By examining the historical context, rhetorical strategies, and emotional appeals within these national eulogies, the project highlights how presidents shape public mourning, foster unity, and set expectations for leadership in times of crisis.
